Two Communities in Florida

St. Augustine

St. Augustine was founded in 1565 and is the oldest western-established settlement in Florida and in the Western Hemisphere.

Ponce de Leon's Fountain of Youth has guest books stretching back to 1868.

The Alligator Farm and Zoological Park were founded in 1893 and has the old living examples of all 23 species of crocodilian.

St. Augustine is repeatedly named as being one of the most haunted cities in America. Ghost stories go back at least 450 years.

The Pirate & Treasure Museum is home to one of the only three remaining authentic Jolly Roger flags known to exist.

The San Sebastian Winery produces over one million bottles of wine each year.

The St. Augustine lighthouse was built in 1874, is 165 feet tall, and is painted with black and white strings with red detailing.

The original Ripley's Believe It or Not! museum is located in St. Augustine. It has over 20,000 square feet and features 19 themed galleries, and 300 exhibits.

Many notable residents of St. Augustine include Ray Charles, Zora Neale Hurston, and Stetson Kennedy.

The St. Augustine Amphitheater is a 4,000 seat outdoor concert venue, and it hosts some of the biggest names in music including James Taylor, Jack Johnson, and Aretha Franklin.

Orlando

Orlando has more than 100 lakes. Lake Eola is a sinkhole that is 80 feet deep.

Orlando was the main hub of Florida's citrus industry. A deep freeze in the late 1800s caused farmers to move their citrus crops further south.

The city was originally named Jernigan, after the first permanent settler in the area. Orlando may have been renamed for a character in Shakespeare's "As You Like It"

The Rogers Building and the Railroad Depot are the oldest building remaining in downtown Orlando.

66 million people visited Orlando in 2015 and Orlando is the #1 tourist city in the world. The number of daily visitors to Orlando is the size of the population of Atlanta.

Gatorland is a theme park that was opened in 1949. Its main attraction was a 15-foot gator (a world record).

Universal Studios opened in Orlando in 1990 with Nickelodeon, Back to the Future, E.T. Ghostbusters, and the Bates Hotel as attractions.

The old city hall was blown up as part of the opening scene of Lethal Weapon 3.

The official nickname of Orlando is "The City Beautiful". It is also known as "O-Town".

The Orlando Magic (NBA team) almost became the Orlando Juice. The finalist names were the Heat, the Tropics, the Juice, and the Magic.
